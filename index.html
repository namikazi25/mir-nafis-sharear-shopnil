About

I am an AI researcher and engineer interested in understanding how models coordinate, reason, and make decisions. My work involves building multi-agent systems with large language models, developing interpretable AI architectures, and training foundation models for real-world applications.

I explore how AI systems can exhibit emergent coordination, how we can interpret the mechanisms behind model reasoning, and how reinforcement learning can incorporate bounded rationality and human-like decision making. My recent projects include agentic misinformation detection systems using graph neural networks and LLM orchestration for climate adaptation workflows.

I hold an MSc in Artificial Intelligence from Queen Mary University of London and a BSc in Computer Science from BRAC University. I currently work as an AI Engineer at Technovative Solutions and Research Fellow with the Fatima Fellowship.

Research Interests

  • Multi-agent systems and collective intelligence in LLMs
  • Reinforcement learning with cognitive constraints and bounded rationality
  • Mechanistic interpretability and understanding model reasoning processes
  • Graph-based representations for relational and structured reasoning
  • Training foundation models for reliable real-world deployment
